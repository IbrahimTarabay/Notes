Compound Learning

The idea comes from compound interest, the idea of compound learning is it leverages the fact that learning occurs every day in our life, we want to shift our focus from just cramming as much information as we want in a huge full day learning session into just small little chunks, The idea is to make small changes every single day

-->Improve by 1% a day, and in 70 days you're twice as good

Pareto Principle

-->also known as the 80/20 rule, it's the idea that roughly 80% of the results usually comes from 20% of the causes or inputs

ex:
80% of sales comes from 20% of clients
80% of code comes from 20% of the programmers

-->The closer we get to 100% mastery the longer and longer it takes but usually the initial 80% can be achieved quite fast and this principle is what we're doing
